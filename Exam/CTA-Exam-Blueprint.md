# CTA Beta Exam Blueprint

**Source:** KB0013442 — Updated April 2026  
**Status:** Work in progress, subject to ongoing updates

---

## Exam Purpose

The Certified Technical Architect Beta Exam confirms mastery in designing scalable, sustainable architecture solutions on the ServiceNow platform. Topics covered:

- CMDB and CSDM
- Technical governance
- Testing leading practices
- Go-live preparation
- Security architecture
- Data strategies
- Platform data and integrations
- Release and instance strategy
- Current and to-be architecture planning

Passing awards a CTA Certification and a Credly digital badge.

---

## Intended Audience

- Minimum 3 years hands-on ServiceNow implementation experience
- Demonstrated ability to translate business requirements into platform capabilities
- Must be in an accepted state in the CTA program

---

## Prerequisites

- Review the Certified Technical Architect Website for prerequisites
- Complete the CTA class (waived for beta exam participation; required before certification is awarded)

---

## Exam Structure

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | 240 minutes (4 hours) |
| Number of Questions | ~180 |
| Question Format | Use case + multiple-choice (single answer) |
| Result Timing | 6–8 weeks after beta window closes |

### Question Types

- **Use Case:** Short passage describing a realistic situation (business requirement, technical constraint, organizational challenge). All questions are tied to a use case.
- **Scenario-Based Question:** Builds on the use case with additional real-world context. Candidates analyze, identify relevant info, apply best practices.
- **Multiple Choice (Single Answer):** At least 3 options; select the one best answer.

## Exam Domains and Weightings

| # | Domain | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Requirements and Stakeholder Alignment | 17% |
| 2 | Architecture and Design | 33% |
| 3 | Governance and Lifecycle | 17% |
| 4 | Communication and Influence | 25% |
| 5 | Scalability | 8% |
| | **Total** | **100%** |

---

### Domain 1 — Requirements and Stakeholder Alignment (17%)

*Given a use case:*

- Analyze current-state versus desired-state requirements to identify architectural gaps and implications
- Evaluate and align solution requirements with stakeholders using demos or prototypes

---

### Domain 2 — Architecture and Design (33%)

*Given a use case:*

- Analyze client requirements to architect scalable, secure, upgradable, and maintainable ServiceNow solutions
- Assess trade-offs between client needs and platform best practices to recommend sustainable solutions
- Develop technical specifications that reflect architectural decisions and platform constraints
- Analyze solution components to identify reuse and optimization opportunities across the platform

---

### Domain 3 — Governance and Lifecycle (17%)

*Given a use case:*

- Apply architectural governance principles to ensure adherence to standards and regulations
- Assess alignment of ServiceNow architecture with enterprise IT strategy and roadmap

---

### Domain 4 — Communication and Influence (25%)

*Given a use case:*

- Analyze and communicate architectural trade-offs and risks in business terms to stakeholders
- Formulate an architecture rationale that supports executive decision-making and strategic alignment
- Evaluate competing priorities to negotiate solutions that balance business and technical constraints

---

### Domain 5 — Scalability (8%)

*Given a use case:*

- Analyze performance bottlenecks and apply optimization strategies for scalable architecture
