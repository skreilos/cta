# Week 4 - Weekly Quiz

> **Quiz**
> **Q:** Which of the following statements accurately reflect the Agile mindset and its foundational values? (Select all that apply)
> **Options:**
> - Promote adaptability and collaboration over rigid, plan-driven methods
> - Emphasize comprehensive documentation over working software
> - Prioritize individuals and interactions over processes and tools
> - Focus on plans and contracts over responding to change
> - Prioritize project management methodologies over iterative development
> **Correct:** Promote adaptability and collaboration over rigid, plan-driven methods, Prioritize individuals and interactions over processes and tools
> **Erklärung:** The Agile Manifesto values adaptability, collaboration, individuals and interactions, working software, responding to change, and customer collaboration — not documentation, fixed plans, or rigid methodologies.

> **Quiz**
> **Q:** Which of the following statements correctly highlights the difference between Agile and Waterfall methodologies?
> **Options:**
> - Agile relies on fixed scope and timeline with siloed project teams, while Waterfall follows a flexible scope with cross-functional agile teams.
> - Agile emphasizes title-based roles, while Waterfall supports interconvertible roles and investment in coaching.
> - Agile follows deliverable-based work structures, while Waterfall focuses on outcome-based delivery.
> - Agile promotes fixed timeline with flexible scope and cross-functional agile teams, while Waterfall follows fixed scope and timeline with siloed project teams.
> **Correct:** Agile promotes fixed timeline with flexible scope and cross-functional agile teams, while Waterfall follows fixed scope and timeline with siloed project teams.
> **Erklärung:** Agile = fixed timeline + flexible scope + cross-functional teams. Waterfall = fixed scope & timeline + siloed teams.

> **Quiz**
> **Q:** A multinational telecom company is rolling out a large-scale ServiceNow implementation involving more than 100 Agile team members distributed across regions. Each team manages its own backlog but the enterprise needs an organization-wide framework to ensure alignment, collaboration, and delivery across multiple value streams, maintaining transparency and built-in quality. Which Agile framework would best fit this requirement?
> **Options:**
> - Scrum
> - Scrum of Scrums
> - SAFe
> - Hybrid
> **Correct:** SAFe
> **Erklärung:** SAFe is designed for large-scale implementations (50+ people) with multiple value streams. Its core values are alignment, built-in quality, transparency, and program execution.

> **Quiz**
> **Q:** What is the main purpose of the Common Service Data Model (CSDM) when managing digital products and services with respect to the Configuration Management Database (CMDB)?
> **Options:**
> - Defines the automation logic that is used to synchronize CMDB data across multiple instances
> - Replaces CMDB by creating a new database structure for service data
> - Provides a standardized framework that ensures data resides in the appropriate CMDB tables for improved quality and insights
> - Limits the number of configuration items stored in the CMDB to optimize performance
> **Correct:** Provides a standardized framework that ensures data resides in the appropriate CMDB tables for improved quality and insights
> **Erklärung:** CSDM is a blueprint and framework for where data belongs in the CMDB — it does not replace the CMDB.

> **Quiz**
> **Q:** True or False: In the CSDM conceptual model, the Design domain represents the tables that are used to rationalize and manage business applications, though these tables cannot be used for Incident or Change Management.
> **Options:**
> - True
> - False
> **Correct:** True
> **Erklärung:** The Design domain tables are not operational — they cannot be selected for Incident Management or Change Management.

> **Quiz**
> **Q:** A customer reports that their CMDB contains inconsistent and incomplete configuration data from multiple sources. They want to improve data accuracy and ensure that updates, deletions, and merges happen automatically during imports. Which CMDB management pillar should the customer focus on to address this challenge?
> **Options:**
> - Ingestion – Manages continuous data intake and consolidation from various sources into a reliable model
> - Governance – Defines roles, responsibilities, and processes for CMDB lifecycle management
> - Insight – Establishes reporting metrics and dashboards for service performance
> - Discovery – Automates data reconciliation and merging during imports
> **Correct:** Ingestion – Manages continuous data intake and consolidation from various sources into a reliable model
> **Erklärung:** The Ingestion pillar handles continuous data intake including merging, updating, and deleting data from multiple sources into a single reliable model.
