# Week 3 - Quiz Questions

> **Quiz**
> **Q:** Governance is a decision-making framework that accelerates outcomes by defining which of the following? Select all that apply.
> **Options:**
> - What decisions need to be made and when
> - Who is involved in decision-making
> - How the work gets done
> - How decisions are made
> **Correct:** What decisions need to be made and when, Who is involved in decision-making, How decisions are made
> **Erklärung:** Organizations accelerate outcomes by defining what decisions need to be made and when, who is involved, and how decisions are made. "How the work gets done" is not part of the governance framework — that belongs to the operating model.

> **Quiz**
> **Q:** Which of the following are key components of the governance framework recommended by ServiceNow? Select all that apply.
> **Options:**
> - Technical governance board
> - Executive steering board
> - Demand board
> - Portfolio governance board
> - Operational oversight board
> - Governance coordination forum
> **Correct:** Technical governance board, Executive steering board, Demand board
> **Erklärung:** The ServiceNow governance framework consists of exactly three boards: Executive Steering Board (strategy), Demand Board (portfolio), and Technical Governance Board (technical). Portfolio governance board, Operational oversight board, and Governance coordination forum are not part of this framework.

> **Quiz**
> **Q:** Which of the following are considered best practices when developing applications in ServiceNow? Select all that apply.
> **Options:**
> - Customize out-of-the-box scripts to match business needs
> - Follow a consistent naming convention across all artifacts
> - Use global scope for all applications to ensure broad access
> - Use established and officially supported patterns when working with ServiceNow
> **Correct:** Follow a consistent naming convention across all artifacts, Use established and officially supported patterns when working with ServiceNow
> **Erklärung:** Developers should follow consistent naming conventions and use officially supported patterns. Customizing OOTB scripts increases technical debt and upgrade risk. Global scope should be avoided in favor of scoped applications.

> **Quiz**
> **Q:** Which of the following are best practice approaches for achieving continuous improvement in the governance process? Select all that apply.
> **Options:**
> - Analyze metrics to continuously drive process improvement and remove bottlenecks
> - Define metrics that measure the effectiveness of your governance process
> - Triage decisions that require the attention of governance boards and strive to push all but the most important decisions to your front lines
> - Conduct a thorough review of product documentation and release notes to identify what technical governance policies may be needed
> **Correct:** Analyze metrics to continuously drive process improvement and remove bottlenecks, Define metrics that measure the effectiveness of your governance process, Triage decisions that require the attention of governance boards and strive to push all but the most important decisions to your front lines
> **Erklärung:** Continuous governance improvement focuses on defining metrics, removing bottlenecks, and triaging decisions. Reviewing product documentation is useful for technical governance but is not one of the three core continuous improvement approaches.

> **Quiz**
> **Q:** Which statements best describe the value of investing in effective ServiceNow governance? Select all that apply.
> **Options:**
> - It aligns ServiceNow initiatives and investments with the organization's strategic goals and objectives.
> - It establishes clear decision-making processes and accountability, enabling faster and more effective outcomes.
> - It helps maintain platform integrity through consistent standards, secure practices, and reliable services.
> - It helps organizations prioritize and allocate resources in a way that minimizes value and support for business needs.
> - It ensures quick implementation of new workflows without emphasizing governance structures or controls.
> **Correct:** It aligns ServiceNow initiatives and investments with the organization's strategic goals and objectives., It establishes clear decision-making processes and accountability, enabling faster and more effective outcomes., It helps maintain platform integrity through consistent standards, secure practices, and reliable services.
> **Erklärung:** Options 1, 2, and 3 reflect the three pillars of governance value: strategic alignment, faster decision-making, and platform integrity. Option 4 is a trap — "minimizes value" is the opposite of what governance does. Option 5 contradicts governance principles entirely.

> **Quiz**
> **Q:** Your customer is planning to establish a technical governance board to oversee their ServiceNow platform. As a CTA, how should you support the customer in establishing an effective technical governance board?
> **Options:**
> - Manage day-to-day development tasks for the customer to reduce their workload
> - Replace the customer's governance decisions to ensure compliance with ServiceNow capabilities
> - Outline the board's charter, scope, roles, and responsibilities and provide guidance on platform architecture, scalability, and maintainability
> - Implement coding standards and security policies defined by the executive steering board
> **Correct:** Outline the board's charter, scope, roles, and responsibilities and provide guidance on platform architecture, scalability, and maintainability
> **Erklärung:** The CTA's role is advisory: help define the board's structure and provide architectural expertise. The CTA does not manage day-to-day tasks, replace governance decisions, or implement policies on behalf of another board.

> **Quiz**
> **Q:** When defining technical governance policies for ServiceNow, which action best reflects how a CTA ensures the platform stays scalable, secure, and aligned with organizational goals?
> **Options:**
> - Encourage teams to adopt custom integrations and scripts if they meet immediate business needs
> - Define technical standards, such as naming conventions, access levels, and upgrade policies, and ensure they are consistently applied across all environments
> - Delegate governance decisions for data, environment, and platform management to individual developers familiar with those areas
> - Limit governance to architectural design decisions and exclude operational practices like patching or access management to avoid complexity
> **Correct:** Define technical standards, such as naming conventions, access levels, and upgrade policies, and ensure they are consistently applied across all environments
> **Erklärung:** Governance requires consistent standards across all environments and domains — including operational practices. Delegating to individuals, encouraging custom scripts, or limiting scope all undermine scalability and security.

> **Quiz**
> **Q:** Which statement best explains the relationship between the executive steering, demand, and technical governance boards when launching a new customer-facing app?
> **Options:**
> - The executive steering board manages technical integration, while the demand board sets the long-term business vision and the technical board allocates resources.
> - The executive steering board ensures alignment with business goals, the demand board balances resources and timelines, and the technical board defines development feasibility and standards.
> - The technical board is solely responsible for all decisions, with the executive steering and demand boards providing optional input.
> - The demand board develops the app independently, consulting the other boards only if problems arise.
> **Correct:** The executive steering board ensures alignment with business goals, the demand board balances resources and timelines, and the technical board defines development feasibility and standards.
> **Erklärung:** Each board operates at its own level: ESB = strategy and business alignment, Demand Board = portfolio, resources, and timelines, TGB = technical feasibility and standards. Option 1 scrambles the roles; options 3 and 4 break the collaborative model.

> **Quiz**
> **Q:** True or False: Lagging indicators reflect successful governance against the top-level program KPIs; however, leading indicators are more useful for evaluating how effectively governance processes and policies are functioning within the organization.
> **Options:**
> - True
> - False
> **Correct:** True
> **Erklärung:** Lagging indicators measure outcomes against top-level KPIs but are hard to attribute directly to governance. Leading indicators measure governance process effectiveness over time (e.g. board decisions per quarter, % policy updates, escalation reduction) and give earlier, actionable feedback.

> **Quiz**
> **Q:** Which of the following should be a primary priority for governance boards to ensure continuous improvement of the ServiceNow platform implemented for a customer?
> **Options:**
> - Delegating platform decisions primarily to IT administrators
> - Focusing on user interface design enhancements
> - Collecting feedback, optimizing performance, managing change, and monitoring trends
> - Reducing governance activities to accelerate delivery timelines
> **Correct:** Collecting feedback, optimizing performance, managing change, and monitoring trends
> **Erklärung:** Continuous improvement requires the full governance cycle: feedback collection, performance optimization, change management, and monitoring. The other options either narrow scope (UI only), misplace responsibility (IT admins), or undermine governance entirely (reducing activities).
