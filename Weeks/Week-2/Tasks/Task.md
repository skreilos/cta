## Case Study: Acme Corporation — Key Facts

### Company Profile
- Multinational professional services & outsourcing company
- Regions: North America, Europe, Asia
- Clients: highly regulated sectors — healthcare, banking, government

### Business Drivers
- Standardized processes and governance across global footprint
- Improved employee experience with reliable performance and region-specific workflows
- Compliance posture: GDPR, data residency rules, government contract requirements
- Balance efficiency, scalability, and flexibility in IT operations

### Current State
- ServiceNow hosted in North America only → latency for Europe and Asia users
- Growing demand for localized workflows, regulatory-specific processes, language support
- Clients and regulators expect proof of data residency and compliance controls
- IT leadership wants simplified governance as company scales

### Decision: Single Global Instance vs. Regional Instances

| | Single Global Instance | Regional Instances |
|---|---|---|
| **Pros** | Centralized governance, simplified upgrades, consistency | Better performance locally, regional compliance, tailored workflows/languages |
| **Risks** | Latency outside NA, limited localization, GDPR/data residency non-compliance risk | Higher maintenance overhead, fragmented governance, more resource demand, multiple support teams |

### CTA Focus
Evaluate both options and recommend the most suitable strategy with:
- Clear trade-offs
- Roadmap outlining the strategy
- Key risks, governance, compliance, and success measures
